# Research: Apple's Attempted Acquisition of Lux Optics (Halide) & Subsequent Lawsuit

**Date**: March 21, 2026

## Summary

Apple held acquisition talks with Lux Optics (makers of Halide, Kino, Spectre, and Orion) in summer 2025 to bolster the iPhone 18 Pro's built-in Camera app. After talks fell through, Apple recruited co-founder Sebastiaan de With, leading to a lawsuit between the co-founders.

## Timeline

- **Summer 2025**: Apple held acquisition discussions with Lux Optics
- **September 2025**: Talks ended; co-founders agreed future Halide updates could increase company value
- **~November 2025**: Apple recruited de With; Sandofsky began investigating de With over alleged fund misuse
- **December 2025**: Sandofsky fired de With from Lux
- **January 2026**: de With joined Apple's design team
- **March 2026**: Sandofsky filed lawsuit

## Lawsuit Details

- **Court**: California Superior Court, Santa Cruz County
- **Plaintiff**: Ben Sandofsky (Lux Optics CEO/co-founder)
- **Defendant**: Sebastiaan de With (co-founder)
- **Apple is NOT named as a defendant**

### Allegations
- de With misused $150,000+ in Lux company funds for personal expenses since 2022
- de With provided confidential Lux materials and source code to Apple
- de With retained confidential materials related to Lux's future product development
- de With kept Lux's Apple Design Award

### Defense Response
- Lawyers call the lawsuit "meritless"
- Deny any Lux IP was disclosed to Apple
- Claim the suit is retaliatory — filed after de With raised concerns about financial irregularities at Lux

## Public Accessibility of Lawsuit

The actual court filing does not appear to be publicly indexed online yet. The original reporting comes from The Information (paywalled). Santa Cruz County Superior Court has a public portal but the case has not surfaced there as of this writing.
